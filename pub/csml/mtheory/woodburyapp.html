Some applications of the Woodbury formula

Recall the Woodbury formula (see these notes):

(Woodbury formula) let \(A\), \(C\) be square invertible matrices of dimensions \(n_A \times n_A\) and \(n_C\times n_C\) respectively, let \(B\) and \(D\) be matrices of size \(n_A\times n_D\) and \(n_D\times n_A\) respectively, then the following identity holds: $$ (A+BCD)^{-1} \quad\!\! =\quad\!\! A^{-1} - A^{-1} B(C^{-1} + DA^{-1} B)^{-1} D A^{-1} $$

Sherman-Morrison formula

Let \(C=1\), \(B=u\) and \(D=v\) with \(u, v\in\mathbb R^{n_A}\) then the formula gives

$$ (A+uv^T)^{-1} \quad\!\! =\quad\!\! A^{-1} -{A^{-1} u v^T A^{-1}\over 1 + v^T A^{-1} u} $$
